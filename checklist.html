Pre-departure game plan

Do these before your flight and thank yourself later.

This is the practical checklist: documents, money, summer travel setup, tech, apps, health items, and final must-do tasks before you head to Japan.

Passport first 0-fee card tips Summer travel setup

Top priorities

  • Passport valid for at least 6 months beyond return.
  • International data plan or eSIM set up before departure.
  • Bank cards checked for fees and travel notices submitted.
  • Carry-on packed with meds, chargers, passport, and valuables.

Travel documents and pre-departure tasks

  • Check your passport early and make sure it is valid for at least 6 months beyond your return date.
  • Make sure your passport has at least 1 to 2 blank pages.
  • Create both a printed and digital copy of your passport.
  • Confirm your flight details and save them on your phone and email.
  • Keep printed copies of your flight itinerary, housing information, and emergency contacts.
  • Share your itinerary and emergency contact details with family.

Good habit

Keep one digital backup folder with your passport copy, itinerary, housing address, and emergency contact list in case your phone battery dies or you lose service.

Money, cards, and phone setup

Phone plan

  • Check whether your phone is unlocked.
  • Check whether your current phone plan includes international data.
  • If not, set up a SIM or eSIM before departure.
  • Popular eSIM choices include Airalo and Ubigi.

Bank cards

  • Bring at least 2 cards, including 1 backup card.
  • Check international ATM fees and foreign transaction fees.
  • Check your daily ATM withdrawal limit.
  • Notify your bank or credit card company that you will be in Japan.
  • Bring some yen for arrival expenses, but plan to use cards and ATMs once there.

Technology essentials

  • Laptop or tablet for coursework and editing.
  • Phone charger and device cables.
  • Portable battery bank.
  • Headphones or earbuds.
  • AirTags or another tracking device for luggage are recommended.

Health, essentials, and final prep

Health items

  • Prescription medications in original containers.
  • Copy of prescriptions if needed.
  • Pain reliever, cold medicine, and basic first-aid items.
  • Sunscreen, refillable water bottle, and toiletries for arrival day.

Final tasks

  • Charge all devices the night before travel.
  • Weigh your luggage before leaving home.
  • Keep passport, wallet, chargers, and meds in your carry-on.
  • Double-check that your most important files are saved offline.
