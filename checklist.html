Pre-departure essentials for Japan

Use this page as your main checklist before departure. Complete these items early so you are ready for travel, coursework, and daily life in Japan.

Top priorities

  • Passport valid for at least 6 months beyond return
  • International phone data plan or eSIM arranged
  • Bank cards checked for fees and travel notice submitted
  • Carry-on packed with medications, chargers, and valuables

Travel documents and pre-departure tasks

  • Check your passport and make sure it is valid for at least 6 months beyond your return date.
  • Make sure your passport has at least 1 to 2 blank pages.
  • Create both a printed and digital copy of your passport.
  • Confirm your flight details and save them digitally.
  • Keep printed copies of your flight itinerary, housing information, and emergency contacts.
  • Share your itinerary and emergency contact details with family.

Important reminder

For students using a U.S. passport, short stays in Japan typically do not require a visa, but students should verify current entry requirements before travel.

Phone, banking, and technology

Phone plan

  • Check whether your phone is unlocked.
  • Check whether your current phone plan includes international data.
  • If not, set up a SIM or eSIM before departure.
  • Recommended eSIM options include Airalo and Ubigi.

Bank cards

  • Bring at least 2 cards, including 1 backup card.
  • Check international ATM usage fees.
  • Check your daily withdrawal limit.
  • Notify your bank or credit card company that you will be in Japan.
  • Use cards with no foreign transaction fees whenever possible.

Technology essentials

  • Bring a laptop for coursework and content creation.
  • Bring your phone charger and device cables.
  • Pack a battery bank or portable charger.
  • Bring headphones or earbuds.
  • AirTags or a similar tracking device are recommended.

Health, medications, and final tasks

Health and medications

  • Bring prescription medications in original containers.
  • Bring a copy of prescriptions.
  • Pack common medications such as Advil, Tylenol, Motrin, or cold medicine if you use them.
  • Carry a refillable water bottle.
  • Wear comfortable walking shoes.

Final pre-departure tasks

  • Charge all devices before travel day.
  • Download offline maps and important files.
  • Double-check your carry-on items.
  • Review program expectations and communication channels.
  • Pack thoughtfully and leave some room for souvenirs.
