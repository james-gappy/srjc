Packing Guide

What to pack and what to keep in your carry-on

This guide combines practical clothing and technology advice with a strong carry-on checklist so students avoid common travel mistakes on departure day.

Carry-on must-haves

  • Passport and wallet
  • Medications
  • Phone, chargers, and batteries
  • Valuables and important documents

Packing categories

Bags

  • Suitcase sized within airline restrictions
  • Personal item or carry-on bag
  • Day bag or backpack
  • Extra foldable bag for souvenirs

Clothing basics

  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • 8 to 10 pairs of socks
  • Underwear for the trip
  • Button-up shirts, T-shirts, flannels, or other layers
  • One nicer outfit if desired

Outerwear

  • Packable rain jacket
  • Light jacket, hoodie, or sweater
  • Hat or beanie depending on weather
  • Thermals if needed

Toiletries and health

  • Toothbrush, toothpaste, and floss
  • Deodorant and face soap
  • Razor and sunscreen
  • Prescription medications
  • Common over-the-counter medications
  • Feminine products if needed

Electronics

  • Computer
  • Battery bank
  • Phone and charging cable
  • Phone tripod for content creation
  • Headphones or earbuds
  • Smartwatch charger if applicable
  • Kindle or camera if desired
  • Plug adapter if needed

Carry-on checklist

Always keep in your carry-on Notes
PassportNever place it in checked luggage
Wallet, money, and cardsKeep them accessible during transit
Prescription medicationsCarry enough for travel delays and keep them in original containers
Chargers and cablesUseful during airport layovers and arrival
Electronics and batteriesPortable batteries should stay in carry-on luggage
ValuablesDo not place expensive items in checked luggage
Important documentsItinerary, housing details, emergency contacts

TSA reminder

Do not bring liquids over 100 ml or 3.4 oz in your carry-on unless they meet security exceptions.

Simple packing advice

  • Pack light and leave room for purchases made abroad.
  • Choose comfort over overpacking.
  • Think about walking, weather, and classroom needs.
  • Lay out your carry-on items separately before departure day.
